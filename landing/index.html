About

Pebblic Speaking is an application built in Simply.js for the Pebble to help public speakers practice their speeches, remember the topics they want to hit, and pace themselves during a speech. The app does this by providing three different services to users:

  • Timed practice
  • Reminders during delivery
  • Basic notecards

Timed practice aims to accomplish two goals: to have the user themselves determine how fast or slow they want to proceed through a speech, and to remember the average time needed by the user. It achieves these two goals by taking a list of key 'topic' or trigger words uploaded by the user beforehand in our handy web form, and tracking when the user either presses a button on the Pebble or flicks their hand to signal that they have moved on to the next topic. This results in a list of topics corresponding to the amount of time spent on each. This data is then uploaded to the server and averaged.

The app then can pull this data to provide reminders during the delivery of the speech to encourage the user to move on to the next topic in time. Each time the app thinks the user should move on to the next topic based on averages from practice, the app makes the Pebble vibrate. The user would then finish up speaking on their current topic and move on to the next one.

Finally, the app can serve as basic notecards for the speaker by providing the topics one after another, accessed one at a time by either a button click or a flick of the Pebble, without a time limit or recording. This is useful for when the speaker knows the topics but not the time they want or need to go through all of them.
